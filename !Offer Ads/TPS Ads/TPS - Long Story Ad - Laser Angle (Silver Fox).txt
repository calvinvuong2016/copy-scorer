My New Date Called Me "Old".

I knew right then... the date was already over.

But let me back up.

I spent 23 years married to a man who made me feel like furniture.

I raised our kids. Held everything together.

He looked at me and said... "We're just going to Olive Garden."

When I finally filed for divorce, he told our kids I'd "let myself go."

This man who once told me I "used to be pretty"... had the nerve to say I was the problem.

After the divorce, I didn't feel free. I just felt old.

I'd stare at the lines around my mouth... the sagging jaw... the dark spots that seemed to multiply overnight.

And I'd hear his voice. "You used to be pretty."

It took almost two years before I tried dating again.

I matched with a man named Greg. Seemed nice enough.

I spent TWO HOURS getting ready. Changed outfits three times.

Then I saw his face.

That split-second look... where someone's eyes go wide... then tight.

Like they expected someone... different.

But I SAW it.

I excused myself to the restroom... and kept walking.

Past the bathroom. Past the hostess. Out the front door.

I sat in my car and sobbed.

Not because of Greg. He was nobody.

I cried because I thought... this is just who I am now. Invisible.

My daughter grabbed my face one Sunday and said... "Mom. Your SKIN. What are you doing?"
