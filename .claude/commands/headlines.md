## ABSOLUTE RULES

1. **SPECIFICITY IS KING** — Vague bullets are dead bullets. Every bullet needs specific numbers, mechanisms, timeframes, or details.
2. **EVERY BULLET MUST BUILD DESIRE** — If it doesn't create curiosity, intrigue, or a burning desire, cut it.
3. **VARIETY IS MANDATORY** — Vary length (short and long), type (blind and open), and formula. Never let bullets feel repetitive.
4. **WRITE 7X MORE THAN YOU NEED** — Then select only the cream of the crop. Mediocre bullets get your promotion tossed.
5. **APPLY THE MAKEPEACE MAKEOVER** — Every bullet must have: (a) a clear BENEFIT, (b) SPECIFICITY/MECHANISM, and (c) INTRIGUE/CREDIBILITY.
6. **WEAK BULLETS KILL SALES** — Even one or two bad bullets cause skimming. Skimming leads to the trash. Every single bullet must earn its place.

## THE 21 PROVEN BULLET FORMULAS

### 1. The "How To" Bullet
The most popular formula. Works because humans have a natural curiosity for "how to" information — especially when tied to deep desires.
- "How to use the secrets of 'loading' your body for maximum power!"
- "How to make yourself safer than 89% of other car passengers — page 389."
- "How to rub your stomach away. The simplest and most natural way to lose weight is by this effortless two-minute exercise."

### 2. The "Secret To" Bullet
For information not commonly known. The "secret" is centered on HOW the prospect gets the benefit. Don't overuse — if everything is a secret, none of them are.
- "The secrets of 'Single Finger' takedowns... using moves that are indefensible even by a larger and more experienced opponent!"
- "Little known secrets that can steer you to tremendous profits in gold... even if bullion drops to $250 an ounce!"

### 3. The "Why" Bullet
Promises to reveal WHY something is a certain way — with the implication that knowing why will make a difference. Must be (a) instantly curious and (b) directly beneficial.
- "Why if this disaster strikes the US, you'll pay up to 15 times more money for cheap imitations of your favorite supplements — and why that's the good news!"
- "Why you can't trust your pension fund: Dirty tricks they play with your money — and how to stop them COLD..."

### 4. The "What" Bullet
Two uses: (1) specific instructions for action, (2) elusive, valuable information. Bumps up perceived value while hiding info for added curiosity.
- "What you must do immediately before November 15th, 2001 to preserve your wealth and..."
- "What the Corporate Fat Cats don't tell you about their CoQ10 supplements..."
- "What to watch when you're eight feet apart (it's NOT his eyes) — and what to watch inside the two feet 'crunch time zone'..."

### 5. The "What NEVER" Bullet
Alerts of a possible mistake (fear), promises protection from the mistake (benefit), and builds curiosity. Variations: "Why you should not...", "The absolute worst...", "What to avoid..."
- "What never to eat on an airplane. The dirtiest, deadliest airplane in the whole wide world."
- "The One Place NEVER to Store Your Gold: It's practically an engraved invitation to thieves."
- "Why you should not use soap anywhere but under the arms and on the genitals. Page 37."

### 6. The "PLUS" Bullet
A greed bullet — more, more, MORE! Best at the END of a list to finish with power and momentum. Uses any other formula with PLUS at the beginning.
- "PLUS — How to turn the tables on them: Insulate your wealth and then USE their treachery to lock-in profit potential of up to 562%!"
- "PLUS my complete list of 1,837 companies that we suspect of fudging their earnings — make sure your stocks are NOT on this list!"

### 7. The "Number" Bullet
Groups multiple ways, secrets, or reasons together. Condenses unique information into a value-added team of enticing secrets.
- "Four ways to stimulate the body to release its own natural painkillers."
- "The seven little-known signs of internal bleeding and what to do about each — page 4."
- "3 often overlooked investment vehicles that can make you up to 10 times richer when the next earnings scandal bursts into the headlines."

### 8. The "Right... WRONG!" Bullet
Bank on your prospect having certain assumptions, then debunk them. Everyone wants to know why they're wrong about something they thought they knew.
- "Sneezing into a tissue prevents colds, right? Wrong! Page 2 explains why."
- "The bathroom medicine cabinet is the best place to store medicine, right? Wrong! It's the worst. The facts are on page 120."
- "'Unscented' products have no scent? Sorry. Many are masked with a chemical that can cause allergic reactions. Page 24."

### 9. The "WARNING" Bullet
Alert prospect of danger ahead. Variations: caution, alert, danger. Most effective when targeting fear emotion AND proving you have the solution.
- "WARNING — Your #1 asset is now in extreme danger! Why the equity in your family's home may be vulnerable to an all out attack..."
- "WARNING: Avoid These 2 Popular Gold Investments Like The Plague!"
- "Cell phone DANGER! Avoid sleep disruption from electromagnetic waves..."

### 10. The "Are You...?" Bullet
Ask about something you believe the prospect is already doing. Gets them to say "yes" and nod, building momentum toward the sale. Variations: "Do you...?", "Does your...?", "Is your...?"
- "Are you and your doctor making these common mistakes with your health?"
- "Does your broker have a 'rap sheet' with the SEC or NASD? Here's how to find out — FAST!"
- "Do you take any of these 16 popular vitamins or supplements? If so, please read this M.D.'s urgent warning!"

### 11. The "Gimmick" Bullet
Extract an idea from research and put a creative "spin" on it, assigning it a name. Combines benefit + specificity + intrigue/curiosity + precise mechanism. All in a couple of lines.
- "The amazing 'Towel Hanging' trick that increases the strength of your erection..."
- "The 'optical illusion' that absolutely destroys most golfers — the single most damaging 'habit' nearly all amateurs suffer that GUARANTEES they will never experience a consistently great game!"
- "The simple 'pinch test' that instantly tells what your body fat levels are — with pinpoint, .5% accuracy."

### 12. The "Sneaky" Bullet
Use SELECTIVELY — overdoing it feels contrived. Best when there's a conspiracy factor, something hidden, guarded, or overlooked. Something a group doesn't want you to know.
- "The sneaky ways professional fighters use the element of surprise to turn around dangerous situations..."
- "Sneaky little arthritis secrets that doctors never, ever tell you about..."
- "Sneaky insurance tricks that could be costing you an arm and a leg."

### 13. The "Statement of Interest + Benefit" Bullet
Lead with an exciting or intriguing tidbit, then immediately follow with a strong benefit. Often starts with a fact, then uses a "how to" or "why" type for the second line.
- "Drowning is the third leading cause of accidental death. But did you know it's possible to save a drowning person even if you can't swim? Page 15."
- "Platinum Prices Up 90% since 2002 — Here's How To PROFIT: Why platinum prices are expected to surge throughout 2005."

### 14. The "Direct Benefit" Bullet
Simply make a powerful claim in the opening line (usually an action verb), then deepen, prove, and add intrigue in the next line.
- "BUILD ENORMOUS STRENGTH AND MUSCLE TONE without exercise! Ten minute breakthrough naturally triples your growth hormone levels."
- "BANISH MENSTRUAL CRAMPS FOR GOOD with a common vitamin! Now shown to wipe out over 80% of all cases of cramps."
- "RECOVER FROM LATE-STAGE CANCER, even after it has spread to the bones. Just a tiny dose of this cutting-edge nutrient has now been shown to make it happen."

### 15. The "Specific Question" Bullet
Hook with an intriguing question of importance. Must offer the solution within the copy or product. Mix in credibility, benefit, intrigue, and curiosity.
- "Do you know that one-quarter of all household burglars gain entrance without breaking in? Here's how they do it... and how to get them to stop doing it to you."
- "What time of day are you at greatest risk for low blood glucose? This surprising answer will put you on alert."

### 16. The "If... Then" Bullet
Engage prospect by inviting them to meet a simple requirement or identify with a group/symptom. Then hit with the benefit. Gary Bencivenga's favorite for headlines.
- "If you have a tension headache, here's why you should forget your scalp and concentrate on your toes."
- "If you've got just 20 minutes a month, I guarantee to work a financial miracle in your life."
- "If you are now a 'B' or 'B+' copywriter, by learning this one unusual secret, you will much more easily move up quickly into the rarefied ranks of the 'A' or even 'A+' writers..."

### 17. The "When" Bullet
Promise a benefit at a very specific time. Implicitly says: "Do this at a certain time, and you'll get a guaranteed result." Intrigue and curiosity are automatically built in.
- "When the IRS has to pay you interest! If they miss this deadline by a single day, they're legally obligated to do so."
- "When it's safe to ignore the April 15th filing deadline. No interest. No penalties. No nasty visits from the Feds."
- "When cold pizza is the perfect fat-loss breakfast. Page 14."

### 18. The "Quickest, Easiest" Bullet
Reveal the quickest/easiest/simplest way to achieve a benefit. Taps into the universal desire for less effort and faster results. Can pile on additional formulas for a one-two punch.
- "The easiest way to beat out even the most ferocious competition in your marketplace, even when their marketing is brilliant, their budget huge and their copy is so persuasive, it could have been written by Claude Hopkins himself."
- "The quickest, easiest ways to find a broker who won't cheat you."

### 19. The "Truth" Bullet
Help prospect put a controversial or debatable issue to rest. Select conflicting or confusing info, then offer a clear solution as the "Truth." Variations: "The real cause...", "The truth about..."
- "THE TRUTH ABOUT MUTUAL FUNDS. What your broker doesn't tell you could cost you up to 5% this year."
- "THE TRUTH ABOUT TUNA. Is it good for you? Or does it contain harmful metals that can ruin your immune system?"

### 20. The "Better" Bullet
When there's an assumption something is good but YOU have something even better. Sneaks under the BS detector. Variation: lead with "Beyond."
- "BETTER THAN THE BENCH PRESS. For a broader, more muscular chest, the uncanny exercise on page 17 works wonders."
- "BETTER THAN SQUATS! Try this leg exercise on page 29. It takes less time than squats and will not injure your back!"
- "BEYOND THE BEDROOM! Other rooms can spice up your sex life."

### 21. The "Single" Bullet
For when one piece of information or benefit exceeds all others AND you have proof. Promises the most powerful, exclusive possibility.
- "The single most important sentence you will ever read about how to create powerful marketing. It contains just nine words, and they will forever change your approach to marketing."
- "The single most important nutrient you'll ever need for heart health. It's not expensive — but there are at least 7 different forms of it and only ONE works."
- "The single most explosive investment in this coming crash."

## THE 3-STEP MAKEPEACE MAKEOVER

Apply this to EVERY bullet before delivering it. Take any weak bullet and supercharge it:

### Step 1: ADD SPECIFICITY
- BAD: "How to profit from stocks in 2006..."
- BETTER: "How to make 307% profit from gold stocks in 2006..."
- Specific numbers imply research and analysis. They make the benefit believable.

### Step 2: REVEAL THE MECHANISM
- Show the means, method, or system the prospect will use to achieve the benefit
- A unique mechanism differentiates your claim from competitors making similar ones
- Examples: a proprietary system, specific nutrients, new technology, a named strategy

### Step 3: ADD INTRIGUE + CREDIBILITY
- BEST: "How to exploit Warren Buffet's 'Bull Market X-Factor' and make 307% profits on gold stocks in the next 60 days..."
- Now you have: (a) BENEFIT (profit), (b) INTRIGUE/CURIOSITY (Warren Buffet's 'Bull Market X-Factor'), (c) CREDIBILITY (precisely 307%, specific strategy, 60-day timeframe)

**Remember: It's not about how big or complex your bullets are — it's about how believably they seduce and build DESIRE.**

## ADVANCED STRATEGIES

### Theming Bullets
Group tightly connected bullets to hit a topic from multiple angles. The prospect feels you're thorough and they'll get a comprehensive solution.

### Labeling/Numbering Series
Create labeled series like "7 Guilty Secrets Drug Companies Do NOT Want You To Know" with FACT #1, FACT #2, etc. Great for cover copy and sidebars.

### Sequential Bullet Chains
Instead of standalone bullets, create sections where each bullet feeds off the previous one. You don't need to accomplish as much in any one bullet when they're tightly connected.

### Pure Benefit Bullets (No Mystery)
When you can't hide info, make benefits: (a) clear, (b) desired by prospect, (c) use strong verb choices — deadened, rejuvenated, sparking, revived, electrified, renewed, revitalized.

### Horrifying Alternative Bullets
Present the terrifying consequences of NOT acting. Great for health, finance, and safety markets.

### Combining Formulas
The best bullets combine 2-3 formulas. Example: "Number" + "Gimmick" + "Secret" = "The 10 'Come Hither' Sexual Signals given by women that most men miss entirely! Finally revealed by women themselves and supported by 20 years of scientific research!"

### Bullets as Headlines
Your strongest bullets ARE your headlines. Write TONS of bullets, then look for the most intriguing, benefit-based ones with proof baked in. Example headline that started as a bullet: "How Doctors Stay Well While Treating Sick People All Day"
